                Market Overview
The market surrounding ZIP code 34484, within a 15-mile radius, is characterized by steady population growth, a
mature demographic profile, and a strong presence of retirees and older adults. The total population has
increased by over 10% in the past five years, reaching more than 364,000 residents. The area is notably older than
the national average, with large cohorts in the 60-79 age brackets and a high aging index, indicating a significant
proportion of seniors. This demographic trend suggests strong demand for healthcare, senior living, and services
tailored to older adults, while also signaling a relatively lower labor force participation rate and a potential
challenge for businesses reliant on younger workers.

Racially, the market is predominantly White, with smaller but growing Hispanic and Black populations. The
diversity index remains moderate, which may influence the types of cultural products and services that resonate
locally. Median household income has risen to over $66,000, but per capita income is lower, reflecting the
prevalence of retirees and smaller household sizes. The area’s homeownership rate is high, and housing values
have appreciated rapidly, outpacing income growth. This dynamic creates opportunities in real estate, home
improvement, and financial services, but also raises affordability concerns for younger families and workers.

Vehicle ownership is robust, with an average of 1.6 vehicles per household and a total registered vehicle count
exceeding 348,000. Light trucks are especially popular, and domestic brands have a strong presence. Commute
times are moderate, averaging 23 minutes, and most residents drive to work, with limited use of public
transportation. This car-centric culture supports demand for automotive sales, maintenance, and related services,
but also means that businesses dependent on foot traffic or public transit may face challenges.

The insurance and collision repair market is active, with over 350 claims reported and a handful of certified body
shops serving the area. The relatively high number of accidents and claims, combined with the large vehicle fleet
and older population, suggests ongoing demand for insurance, repair, and related services. However, the limited
number of certified shops may indicate an opportunity for expansion or new entrants, especially as the population
continues to grow and age. For businesses, these trends highlight the importance of catering to an older, vehicle-
dependent population with rising incomes but also increasing cost pressures, and suggest that services related to
healthcare, home, and automotive needs are likely to see sustained demand.
