                Market Overview
The market within a 15-mile radius of ZIP code 32726 is characterized by steady population growth, increasing
diversity, and a demographic profile that skews older than the national average. The total population stands at
over 263,000, with a growth rate of nearly 9.5% over five years, indicating a healthy influx of residents and
potential customers 1 2 . The racial composition is predominantly White, but there are significant Black and
Hispanic communities, and a diversity index above 33% suggests a gradually broadening cultural mix 3 1 . The
aging index is high, with a large proportion of residents over 60, which can influence demand for healthcare,
home services, and products tailored to seniors 4 1 .

Income levels are moderate, with a median household income just under $70,000 and a per capita income of
about $31,000 5 6 . While these figures are below some urban benchmarks, they are paired with a relatively high
homeownership rate of nearly 79% and a median home value that has risen sharply to over $240,000 in recent
years 5 7 . Housing costs have increased, but the home value-to-income ratio remains manageable, suggesting
continued affordability for many families. However, the area does face a rental burden rate above 54% and a
mortgage burden rate over 104%, which could signal financial stress for some segments and opportunities for
affordable housing solutions 5 .

Vehicle ownership is high, with an average of 1.79 vehicles per household and a registered vehicle count
exceeding 250,000 8 . The market is dominated by personal vehicles, with light trucks and cars making up the vast
majority of registrations, and domestic brands like Ford and Chevrolet leading the pack 9 10 11 . Commute
times average 25 minutes, reflecting a suburban or exurban lifestyle where driving is essential 8 . Most residents
drive to work alone, and public transportation usage is negligible, which means businesses reliant on foot traffic or
transit commuters may face challenges, while those catering to drivers—such as auto repair, gas stations, and
drive-thru services—are well positioned 12 .

The insurance and auto repair landscape is competitive but not saturated. There are only a handful of certified
body shops in the area, with just two in Eustis and a few others scattered across nearby towns 13 . Over 1,500
insurance claims were processed recently, with State Farm, GEICO, and Progressive being the most active providers
 14 15 . The average body and paint hours per claim are in line with industry norms, but the relatively low
number of certified shops suggests an opportunity for new entrants, especially those offering specialized or high-
quality services. The combination of high vehicle ownership, long commutes, and a growing, aging population
points to sustained demand for automotive services, insurance, and related businesses. However, rising housing
costs and pockets of financial strain may require businesses to be sensitive to price and value, while the increasing
diversity and older demographic present opportunities for tailored marketing and product offerings 1 5 3 4 .
