## **All County Collision: Company Overview**

### **Purpose**

This internal document provides a comprehensive overview of All County Collision, including the company’s background, business identity, leadership context, facility footprint, customer profile, market footprint, repair philosophy, certifications, operational capability, and growth direction. It is intended for internal team reference and as structured background knowledge for AI systems supporting website content, landing pages, GBP posts, and related marketing work.

This is not public website copy. It is a source of truth internal reference built to help the marketing team and Custom GPT represent the shop accurately and consistently. The written onboarding document should be treated as the authority source if any future source conflicts, while the onboarding transcript adds voice, nuance, and operational context.

### **Company Identity and Background**

All County Collision is a family rooted collision repair business based in Eustis, Florida. The shop traces its origin to 1992, when Ryan DeMarco’s father opened the business. State business records are referenced internally as January 1993, so both dates appear in source material, but the practical identity of the shop is a business with more than three decades of operating history in the local market.

Ryan DeMarco took over leadership in 2016 after growing up around the business and gradually stepping into the work itself. He helped in the detail bay when he was younger, spent time as an apprentice technician, and also worked around prep, disassembly, and reassembly. After returning from college, he expected to learn through estimating first, but moved into management almost immediately. That background matters because All County Collision should not be framed as absentee owned or purely sales driven. It is a shop led by someone who understands production realities and continues to push the business toward stronger process control, better OEM alignment, and long term investment.

### **Brand Direction and Business Identity**

The business should be branded as All County Collision, not “All County Collision and Repair,” moving forward. Ryan does not want the company framed as a general mechanical repair business. The brand direction is toward a focused collision identity built around quality repair, OEM procedure discipline, customer guidance, and modern technical capability.

At a business identity level, All County Collision should be understood as a quality first independent collision center serving customers who care about safety, proper repair standards, and a better repair experience. It is not positioned as the cheapest shop, and it should not be marketed like one.

This helps clarify that the business is not simply chasing any lead volume. The quality and fit of the work mix matter.

### **Target Customer Profile and Market Footprint**

All County Collision’s ideal customer is someone who cares about the safety and quality of their vehicle and is willing to value a correct repair over the lowest possible price. In practical terms, this often maps to newer model vehicle owners, customers with more concern about proper parts and procedures, and people who want guidance rather than a rushed transactional experience.

The shop’s current market footprint includes Eustis along with surrounding communities such as Tavares, Umatilla, Mount Dora, Sorrento, and Leesburg, plus smaller nearby areas including Dona Vista, Grand Island, Altoona, Haines Creek, Deer Island, Bassville Park, Silver Lake, Howey in the Hills, Yalaha, and Weirsdale. Eustis is the anchor, but the business should not be treated as serving only one city in isolation.

The Oxford expansion adds another layer to that market story. Ryan describes the Villages area as a large retirement community tied together by golf cart access, with a distinct customer profile and convenience opportunities such as planned pickup and delivery for residents.

### **Operational Model and Customer Experience Philosophy**

All County Collision’s service model is more structured than a typical “write an estimate and wait” collision shop. First contact leads to either an in person inspection or a photo estimate workflow. If the vehicle is not drivable, the shop may take it in right away. If it is drivable, the team works to manage the repair in advance through preliminary estimating, insurance approvals when applicable, pre ordering parts, and in some cases early damage discovery planning.

Once the vehicle is dropped off, the workflow moves through a pre wash and check in phase, pre scan and ADAS planning, disassembly for damage discovery, approvals, parts procurement, mirror matching and test fitting, body and structural repair as needed, department handoff quality control, prep and refinish, another handoff quality check, reassembly, sublet steps where needed, post scan and ADAS work, detailing, final QC, and often a test drive before delivery.

The key point is not just that the shop has a process. It is that the process is controlled. The business emphasizes customer communication throughout the repair through automated updates and manual calls, and it uses multiple QC checkpoints between major stages. This should be treated as a foundational company trait, not a throwaway talking point.

### **Repair Standards, Technical Capability, and Certifications**

All County Collision’s repair posture is built around full repair planning and OEM procedure discipline. Internally, Ryan describes a real process for this: the shop pulls full repair plans, researches OEM procedures through approved information sources, and assigns responsibility for verifying that required steps are actually followed. That means “we follow OEM procedures” is tied to a repair planning role and to quality control checks between stages, not just marketing language.

The shop’s technical capability supports that message. Core systems and equipment include CCC estimating, Autel scanning and diagnostics, a Chief S21 frame rack, Car O Liner Vision measuring, Pro Spot welding equipment, Axalta Cromax XP paint, two heated Garmat downdraft booths, and in house ADAS recalibration capability using the Autel IA900. Together, these systems position the shop as capable of handling modern collision complexity rather than only basic cosmetic repair work.

Credentialing reinforces the company’s technical identity. All County Collision is an I CAR Gold Class shop with I CAR Platinum technicians, and it holds OEM certifications for FCA, Nissan, Infiniti, Hyundai, Genesis, and Kia. These should be understood as both trust signals and evidence of the shop’s broader direction toward manufacturer aligned repair standards and training investment.

### **Insurance Positioning, Work Mix, and Competitive Reality**

All County Collision works with insurance companies, but its internal identity is not that of an insurer controlled operation. The shop has a State Farm DRP relationship representing roughly 35 percent of its work, yet Ryan’s own framing is clearly customer first. He emphasizes that the shop works for the customer, not for an insurer’s cost or cycle time incentives, and that the company will not skip steps simply to be cheaper or faster.

The shop is also selective about the work it wants. It welcomes collision repair, fleet work, light in house PDR, in house ADAS, and a mix of in house and sublet mechanical support. At the same time, it does not want to be known for classic restorations, partial repairs, oversized units, boats, RVs, or fiberglass heavy work. That selectivity supports a stronger identity and helps keep the business aligned with the kind of repair work it is set up to perform well.

Ryan also has a visible local presence through family life and coaching involvement, which adds to the impression of a genuinely local, owner led operation rather than a faceless corporate repair center.

The safest internal framing is that All County Collision is a long standing, family rooted Eustis business with practical community involvement and an owner who is active both locally and in the wider collision repair industry.
